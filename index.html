My Journey

My path into computer science wasn't linear, but every step has taught me something valuable.

How I Got Here

I've always been fascinated by how things work. As a child, I would take apart electronics (much to my parents' dismay) just to see the components inside. This curiosity naturally evolved into an interest in programming when I built my first website at 14.

In high school, I joined the robotics team and discovered the thrill of solving complex problems with code. That experience solidified my decision to pursue computer science in college.

What I've Learned

My journey has taught me that:

  • Failure is just feedback - every bug fixed is a lesson learned
  • Collaboration produces better solutions than working alone
  • The most elegant code is often the simplest
  • Continuous learning is essential in technology

Looking Forward

I'm particularly interested in the intersection of AI and healthcare, and how we can leverage technology to solve real-world problems. After graduation, I hope to work on meaningful projects that make a positive impact on people's lives.

When I'm not coding, you can find me on the basketball court, with a camera capturing nature, or volunteering in the community.
